\textbf{Re: Chief Executive Officer --- Fire \& Rescue NSW (Unsolicited)}

\vspace{8pt}
Dear Commissioner Stanton,

The best opportunities rarely come with a job advertisement. This letter is one of them.

I have recently completed a brief strategic audit of Fire \& Rescue NSW and want to be direct with you: I have significant concerns. FRNSW is deploying world-class operational capability at moments of peak customer emotional engagement --- and leaving value on the table. Fires are extinguished. Invoices are not issued. This is not a sustainable model.

I propose a tiered service architecture I call Fire-as-a-Service, or FaaS. Basic smoke alarm response: free, funded by brand awareness. Residential structure fire: Standard tier. High-rise, industrial, or heritage-listed: Enterprise tier, with SLA guarantees. The Rural Fire Service volunteer network represents an untapped contractor workforce that has never been properly platformised. You don't need to own the hoses. Uber doesn't own the cars. Airbnb doesn't own the rooms.

There is also a brand opportunity that cannot be ignored. FRNSW is generating extraordinary awareness at moments of peak emotional engagement. Where is the merch? Where is the app? Where is the community? These are not rhetorical questions. I have answers.

In 2021 I attended a four-day wilderness leadership immersion in the Blue Mountains. I came back different. That experience --- and the proprietary framework it produced --- informs everything about how I lead. I have a 90-day plan for FRNSW. It is ambitious but achievable. I would welcome the opportunity to walk you through it.

I am not looking for a job. I am looking for a mission. And I believe Fire \& Rescue NSW is that mission.

Yours sincerely,

\vspace{8pt}
\textbf{Brad Widejaw, MBA}

\vspace{8pt}
\textit{P.S. Should the CEO conversation need time to marinate, I would also consider a Chief Transformation Officer or Chief Disruption Officer mandate as an interim step.}
